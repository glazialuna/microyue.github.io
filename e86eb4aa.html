example 1 : hiring problem:

  • case 1:

    • Interview for N days, Interview Cost=$C_i$ $\ll$ Hiring Cost=$C_h$, assume M people was hired
    • Total Cost: $O(NC_i + MC_h)$ , Worst Case: M=N
    • Random Order: $O(C_hInN + NC_i)$
    • Permute Array: A[i].P=1+rand()%(N^3)
    • Prove:
      • $E[X_i]$=Pr[candidate is hired]
        $$
        \begin{split}
        E[X]= \sum_{j==1}^N(j*Pr[X=j]) \\
        E[X]=E[\sum_{i=1}^N X_i] = \sum_{i=1}^NE[X_i] \\
        =\sum_{i=1}^N=InN+O(1) \\
        time = O(C_hInN + NC_i)
        \end{split}
        $$

  • case 2: Online(Hire only once)

    • The probability for hire the best qualified candidate, is $\cfrac{1}{e}$ when k=$\cfrac{N}{e}$
    • Prove:
      • $S_i$:the $i^{th}$ applicant is the best
      • {A:= the best one is at position i} $\cap$ {B:=no one at position k+1 ~ i-1 are hired}

$$
\begin{align}
Pr[S_i] &= Pr[A \times B] = Pr[A] \times Pr[B] \\
&= \frac{1}{N} \frac {k}{i-1} \\
&= \frac {k}{N(i-1)} \\
Pr[S] &= \sum_{i=k+1}^{N}Pr[S_i] \\
&=\sum_{i=k+1}^{N}\frac {k}{N(i-1)} = \frac {N}{k} \sum_{i=k}^{N-1} \frac {1}{i}\\
\end{align}
$$
We can find that
$$ \int_k^N\frac{1}{x}dx \leq \sum_{i=k}^{N-1} \frac{1}{x}dx \leq \int_{k-1}^{N-1}\frac{1}{x}dx
$$
thus,
$$
\frac{k}{N}\ln(\frac{N}{k}) \leq \frac {k}{N}In(\frac {N}{k}) \leq \frac{k}{N}\ln(\frac{N-1}{k-1} )
$$
For a given k, we can find the maximum value of $f(k)=\frac{k}{N}\ln(\frac{N}{k})$ is $\frac{1}{e}$

example 2: Quicksort

  • worst running time: $\Theta(N^2)$
  • average case running time: $\Theta(N\log N)$, assuming every permutation is eually likely
  • Prove
    • Pr[Find a central splitter] = $\frac{1}{2}$
    • $Type_j$ : the subproblem is of type j if $N(\frac{3}{4})^{j+1} \leq |S| \leq N(\frac{3}{4})^j$
    • $E[T_{type_j}]=O(N(\frac{3}{4})^j)\times(\frac{4}{3})^{j+1}$
    • Number of different types = $\log_{\frac{4}{3}}N = O(\log N)$

WD(work depth)

  • Measuring
    • W(n) , T(n)[PRAM] , P(n) = $\frac{W(n)}{T(n)}$
    • Sufficiency Theorem: O($\frac{W(n)}{p} + T(n)$) at CW in WD
    • Example 1:summary
      • W(n) = 2n
      • T(n) = logn +@
    • Example 2:Prefix-Sums
      Input: A(i), i=1,… ,n
      Output: $\sum_{i=1}^kA(i)$ ,k=1,2,… ,n
      • T(n)=O(logn) , W(n)=O(n), p $\leq$ n
    • Example 3:Merge
      Merge non-decreasing A(i),B(i)
      Assume:
      logn and n/logn are integers
      n=m, A,B distinct
      • RANK(j,A)=i , if A(i) < B(i) < A(i+1) ,for 1 $\leq$ i $\leq$ n
      • Solve RANK(i,A) and RANK(i,B)
        Binary Search: T(n) = O(logn), W(n)=O(nlogn)
        Serial Rank: T(n)=W(n)=O(n+m)
        Partition: T=O(logn), W=O(plogn)=O(n),p=$\frac{n}{logn}$
      • Solve Place:
        T(n)=O(1) , W(n)=O(n+m)
    • Example 4:Maximum Finding
      • Similar to summation: T(n)=O(1),W(n)=O($n^2$),p=$n^2$
      • Doubly-logarithmic : T=O(loglogn),W=O(n)
        Prove:
        • Step 1: part to h(h=loglogn),give out $\frac{n}{h}$ M
          total: T = (h1) , W = (h$\frac{n}{h}$) = O(n)
        • Step 2: part each to doubly logarithmic tree,tree of leave $\frac{n}{h}$
          each level part to $\sqrt{n}$ T(n)=O(1) , W=O(n)
          total: T(n)=O(loglogn * 1), W=O(nloglogn)
        • total:
          T=O(h + loglog(n/h))=O(loglogn)
          W=O(h*(n/h) + (n/h)loglog(n/h))=O(n)
      • Random : T(n)=O(1), W(n)=O(n)
      • reference: P55,Randm + Doubly algorithm
